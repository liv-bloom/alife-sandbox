## 1. Bounded Coordination Context
**Constraint 1 (Compute Limit):** The NCA must update its grid state within a strict computational budget.
**Constraint 2 (Local Interventions):** External interventions (damage, pruning, or feeding) disrupt the local coordination of cells. The cells must coordinate using only local communication (neighbor states) to repair the global pattern.

## 2. Perceptual Asymmetry
Individual cells within the NCA grid only perceive their immediate neighbors. They have no global awareness of the overall structure they are forming. The perceptual asymmetry lies in the fact that the observer (external agent) sees the global pattern, while the constituent cells operate entirely blindly based on local rules.

## 3. Cultural Emergence Metric
The emergence metric is defined by "Homeostatic Recovery" (the number of steps required to return to equilibrium after severe structural damage) and "Morphological Stability". The collective resilience that emerges from localized updates constitutes a form of primitive structural "culture".

## 4. Failure Log
During early training, when the restriction on update magnitudes was removed (granting cells unlimited restorative power), the NCA exhibited catastrophic explosive growth. Cells over-compensated for minor damage, causing pixel values to saturate and the entire pattern to collapse into chaotic noise within 5 steps. The constraint was essential to maintain the boundary between order and chaos.

## 5. Survival Autonomy
The NCA's survival is tied to a metabolic compute cost. Each update cycle burns "energy". To survive, the organism must learn to minimize unnecessary state changes once the target pattern is reached (entering homeostasis), effectively conserving computational energy (directly analogous to an AI agent's API token usage).
